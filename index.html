Our Legacy

Rooted in the Soil of Bengal

At Saha Swarnalaya, our karigars (artisans) bring centuries-old techniques to life. Located in Midnapore, an ancient hub of culture, we draw inspiration from the terracotta temples of Bishnupur, the intricate scroll-work of Pattachitra artists, and the poetic soul of the Baul mystics.

Every piece of our jewellery is a testament to the unyielding spirit of Bengali folk culture—a harmonious blend of nature's motifs, divine reverence, and unmatched goldsmithing skill.

"Where the golden threads of our history meet the elegance of the modern Bengali woman."

Masterpieces

The Royal Adornments

Sita Haar & Chik

সীতা হার ও চিক

From the magnificent, multi-layered Sita Haar that cascades gracefully down the chest, to the closely fitted, gem-studded Chik (choker), our neckpieces are hallmarks of the Bengali bridal trousseau. Crafted with meticulous 'tar-kaaj' (wirework), they often feature motifs inspired by the Goddess Durga and the lotus flower.

Chur, Mantasha & Bala

চুর, মানতাসা ও বালা

The wrists of a Bengali bride remain incomplete without the heavy, intricately carved gold Chur and the delicate filigree of the Mantasha. Accompanied by traditional Bala (bangles) featuring classic makara (mythological creature) or floral endpoints, our handswear embodies sheer royal heritage.

Kaan Bala & Jhumko

কান বালা ও ঝুমকো

The timeless Kaan Bala is shaped like the crescent curve of the ear, famously inspired by the arched doorways of ancient Zamindar palaces. Paired with the majestic, bell-shaped Jhumko, these earrings gently sway with poetic rhythm, echoing the classical dance forms of Bengal.

Tikli, Nath & Ratanchur

টিকলি ও নথ

The visage of devotion and beauty. Our oversized, regal Nath (nose ring) attached with a delicate gold chain, and the central Tikli resting on the forehead, complete the iconic bridal look. The designs often trace back to the miniature paintings showcasing the divine elegance of Radha-Krishna.
